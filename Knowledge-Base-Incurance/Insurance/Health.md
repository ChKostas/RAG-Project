# HEALTH INSURANCE POLICY – RETAIL

This **Policy** is issued to the **Insured** based on the **Proposal** and declaration together with any statement, report or other document which shall be the basis of this contract and shall be deemed to be incorporated herein, to **Insurer** upon payment of the Premium. This **Policy** records the agreement between **Insurer** and **Insured** and sets out the terms of insurance and the obligations of each party.

> The Policy, the Schedule and any Endorsement shall be read together and any word or expression to which a specific meaning has been attached in any part of this Policy or of Schedule shall bear such meaning whenever it may appear.

> Subject to the terms, Conditions, exclusions and definitions contained herein or endorsed or otherwise expressed hereon, Insurer undertakes to pay the Insured Person the hospitalization expenses arising out of an Injury or Illness/Disease and that are reasonably and necessarily incurred by or on behalf of such Insured Person, but not exceeding the sum Insured for the insured person as mentioned in the schedule of the policy. The following benefits are covered under this policy subject to the sub-limits as stipulated in the policy contract.

## COVERAGE BENEFITS

- Room, Boarding Expenses
- Medical Practitioners fees
- Intensive Care Unit
- Nursing Expenses
- Surgical fees, operating theatre, Anesthetist, Anesthesia, Blood, Oxygen and their administration
- Physiotherapy while being treated as inpatient and being part of the treatment
- Drugs and medicines consumed during hospitalization period
- Hospital miscellaneous services (such as laboratory, X-ray, diagnostic tests)
- Dressing, ordinary splints and plaster casts
- Cost of Prosthetic devices if implanted during a surgical procedure

> Note: Insurer’s Liability in respect of all claims admitted during the period of insurance shall not exceed the Sum Insured for the Insured person as mentioned in the schedule.

# DEFINITIONS

The following words or terms shall have the meaning ascribed to them wherever they appear in this Policy, and references to the singular or to the masculine shall include references to the plural and to the feminine wherever the context so permits:

### Accident
> means a sudden, unforeseen and involuntary event caused by external and visible means.

### Accidental Bodily Injury
> means any accidental physical bodily harm solely and directly caused by external, violent and visible means which is verified and certified by a Medical Practitioner but does not include any sickness or disease.

### Administrator
> means any third party administrator engaged by the Insurer for providing Policy and claims facilitation services to the Insured as well as to the Insurer and who is duly licensed by IRDA for the said purpose.

### Age
> means completed years as at the Commencement Date of the Policy Period.

### Any One Illness
> means any continuous period of illness and includes a relapse within 45 days from the date of discharge from the Hospital/Nursing Home where treatment may have been taken and for which a claim had been made with the Insurer. Occurrence of the same illness after a lapse of 45 days will be considered as a new illness.

### Co-Payment
> means the agreed share of the claim amount which is to be borne by the Insured for each Hospitalisation/claim.

### Day Care Expenses
> means the Reasonable and Customary Expenses incurred towards medical treatment for a Day Care Treatment/Procedure preauthorized by the Administrator and done in a Network Hospital/Day Care Centre, to the extent that such cost does not exceed the Reasonable and Customary Expenses in the locality for the same Day Care Treatment/Procedure.

### Day Care Hospital/Centre
> means a special facility, or an arrangement within a Hospital setting, that enables the patient to come to the Hospital for treatment during the day and return home or to another facility at night.

### Day Care Treatments
> refers to medical treatment, and/or surgical procedure which is:
> - undertaken under General or Local Anaesthesia in a **Hospital** /day care centre in less than 24 hrs because of technological advancement, and
> - which would have otherwise required a **Hospitalisation** of more than 24 hours.
> Treatment normally taken on an out-patient basis is not included in the scope of this definition.

### Diagnostic Centre
> means the diagnostic centers which have been empanelled by Insurer or Administrator as per the latest version of the Schedule of diagnostic centers maintained by Insurer or Administrator, available to the Insured on request.

### Dependent Child/Children
> means children / a child (natural or legally adopted), who are/is financially dependent on the Insured or Proposer aged between 3 months and 23 years and who are unmarried.

### Disease / Illness
> means a condition affecting the general well-being and health of the body that first manifests itself in the Policy Period and which requires treatment by a Medical Practitioner.

### Domiciliary Hospitalisation
> means Medical treatment for a period exceeding three days for such Illness/Disease/Injury which in the normal course would require care and treatment at a Hospital/nursing home but is actually taken whilst confined at home in India under any of the following circumstances:
> - The condition of the patient is such that he/she cannot be removed to the Hospital/nursing Home, or
> - The patient cannot be removed to Hospital/nursing home for lack of accommodation therein.
> Benefits shall not cover:
> - Expenses incurred for pre and post Domiciliary Hospitalisation treatment
> - Expenses incurred for treatment of certain listed diseases (e.g., Asthma, Bronchitis, Chronic Nephritis, Diarrhea, Diabetes Mellitus, Epilepsy, Hypertension, Influenza, Psychiatric Disorders, Tonsillitis, Rheumatism, etc.)

### Eligible Hospitalisation Expenses
> means the expenses which the Insured / Insured Person is entitled for, including applicable room rent and other charges as given in the scope of cover under the policy.

### Epidemic Disease
> means a Disease which occurs when new cases of a certain Disease, in a given human population, during a given period, substantially exceed what is the normal "expected" Incidence Rate based on recent experience.

### Excess
> means the percentage of the sum insured/claim or amount up to which all Expenses covered by this Policy are to be borne by the Insured, and before the liability of the Insurer is commenced.

### External Congenital Anomaly
> means a condition present since birth, in the visible and accessible part of the body, which is abnormal with reference to form, structure or position.

### Family
> means and includes the Insured Person, the Insured Person’s legal Spouse, dependent children, and dependent parents.

### Grace Period
> means the specified period of 15 days immediately following the premium due date during which a payment can be made to renew or continue a Policy in force without loss of continuity benefits such as waiting periods and coverage of Pre-existing conditions/diseases. Coverage is not available for the period for which no premium is received.

### Hospital/Nursing Home
> means any institution established for in-patient care and day care treatment of sickness and/or injuries, registered as a Hospital with local authorities, and under the supervision of a registered and qualified Medical Practitioner, or meeting minimum criteria such as:
> - At least 10 inpatient beds in towns with population less than 10 lakh, and 15 beds in larger towns
> - Qualified nursing staff round the clock
> - Qualified Medical Practitioner(s) in charge round the clock
> - Fully equipped operation theatre

### Hospitalisation
> means the Insured’s admission into Hospital for a continuous period of not less than 24 hours.

### Insured
> means You/Your/Self, the person named in the Schedule, who is a citizen and resident of India, for whom the insurance is proposed and the appropriate premium paid.

### Insured Person
> means the person named in the Schedule / who is a resident of India and for whom the insurance is proposed and premium paid. This includes the Insured Person’s family, including dependent parents.

### Insurer
> means SBI General Insurance Company Limited.

### Inpatient Care
> means care or treatment for which the Insured Person has to be hospitalized for more than 24 hours.

### Intensive Care Unit
> means an identified section, ward or wing of a Hospital under constant supervision of a Medical Practitioner(s), specially equipped for continuous monitoring and treatment of patients in critical condition or requiring life support, with more sophisticated care than in ordinary wards.

### Internal Congenital Anomaly
> means a disease not manifested externally, resulting from congenital disorder due to defects or damage to a developing fetus, possibly due to genetic abnormalities, intrauterine environment, or chromosomal issues.

### Medical Expenses
> mean reasonable & customary expenses unavoidably and reasonably incurred by the Insured for treatment of disease, illness, or injury that may be the subject of a claim as an inpatient, including bed charges, treatment, medicines, consumables, pacemakers, implants, etc., as recommended by the attending Medical Practitioner.

### Medical Practitioner
> means a person holding a valid registration from the medical council of any Indian state, acting within scope of license, including Physician, Specialist, or Surgeon. The Medical Practitioner should not be the Insured or a close family member.

### Mental Illness/Disease
> means any mental disorder or condition characterized by disorganization of personality, mind, and emotions impairing normal psychological, social, or work performance, regardless of cause or origin.

### Network Hospital
> means hospitals/nursing homes listed on a list maintained by the Administrator, which may be amended from time to time.

### Non Network Hospital
> are hospitals/nursing homes outside the network list maintained by the Administrator and Insurer.

### Other Insurer
> means any registered insurer in India other than SBI General Insurance Company Limited.

### Out Patient Department
> means a department where the patient is not hospitalized and is treated in an outpatient setting by a Medical Practitioner.

### Package Service Expenses
> means expenses levied by the Hospital/Nursing Home for treatment of specific surgical procedures/medical ailments as a lump sum, based on tariff schedules.

### Pre-existing Condition
> means any condition, ailment, or injury for which the Insured had signs, symptoms, diagnosis, or received treatment within 48 months prior to the first Policy issued by the Insurer.

### Policy Period
> means the period from the commencement date to the expiry date as stated in the Policy Schedule.

### Post-Hospitalisation Expenses
> means expenses incurred up to 60 days after hospitalization for the same disease/illness/injury, continuing treatment after discharge.

### Pre-Hospitalisation Expenses
> means expenses incurred up to 30 days prior to hospitalization for the same disease/illness/injury, related to the hospitalization.

### Proposal
> means the written application or form filled and signed by the Insured, including any additional information provided.

### Proposer
> means the person providing details in the Proposal form for themselves or another person, and who consents to the terms.

### Qualified Nurse
> means a person holding valid registration from the Nursing Council of India or a State Nursing Council.

### Reasonable and Customary Expenses
> means charges that are:
> - charged for medically necessary treatment, supplies, or services
> - not exceeding usual charges for similar treatment in the locality
> - not incurred if no insurance existed

### Schedule
> means the part of the Policy that details Insured details, cover type, Policy Period, and Sum Insured, including any endorsements.

### Sum Insured
> means the maximum amount payable by the Insurer for claims during the Policy Period, as specified in the Schedule.

### Surgical Operation
> means manual or operative procedures required for treatment of disease, injury, correction of deformities, diagnosis, or cure, performed in a Hospital or day care centre by a Medical Practitioner.

### Waiting Period
> means a period of 30 days from the Policy start date during which claims are not payable, except for accidents. Not applicable for subsequent renewals or hospitalizations due to accidents.

# SCOPE OF COVER

The Insurer shall pay reasonably and necessarily incurred expenses under the following categories, subject to the Sum Insured and deductibles:

1. **Room, Board & Nursing Expenses**
   - Covered up to 1% of Sum Insured per day.
   - ICU charges up to 2% of Sum Insured per day.
   - All admissible claims restricted to maximum 25% of Sum Insured per illness/injury.

2. **Medical Practitioner, Surgeon, Anesthetist, Specialist Fees**
   - Restricted maximum 40% of Sum Insured per illness/injury.

3. **Anesthesia, Blood, Oxygen, Operation Theatre, Surgical Appliances, Medicines & Consumables, Diagnostic Expenses**
   - Restricted maximum 40% of Sum Insured per illness/injury.
   - Charges applicable to the room category chosen; higher room rent options will be limited to the entitled category charges.

4. **Cataract Treatment**
   - Limited to 15% of Sum Insured, maximum INR 25,000 per eye.
   - First two years exclusion for cataract treatment applies.

5. **Pre-Hospitalisation Expenses**
   - Up to 10% of Eligible Hospitalisation Expenses per claim.

6. **Post-Hospitalisation Expenses**
   - Up to 10% of Eligible Hospitalisation Expenses per claim.

7. **Day Care Expenses**
   - Covering surgeries/procedures requiring less than 24 hours hospitalization, with prior approval, up to Sum Insured.

8. **Ambulance Expenses**
   - 1% of Sum Insured per policy period, maximum INR 1500, for licensed ambulance services.

9. **Ayurvedic Medicine**
   - Up to 15% of Sum Insured per policy period, maximum INR 20,000, at registered Ayurvedic hospitals.

10. **Homeopathic & Unani Medicine**
    - Up to 10% of Sum Insured per policy period, maximum INR 15,000, at registered hospitals.

11. **Domiciliary Hospitalisation**
    - Up to 20% of Sum Insured, maximum INR 20,000, for treatment at home as defined.

12. **Organ Donor Expenses**
    - Covering organ extraction costs from a blood relative or approved donor, within the overall Sum Insured.

13. **Free Medical Check-up**
    - For claim-free consecutive 4 years, up to 1% of Sum Insured or INR 2,500.

14. **Parental Care**
    - For persons above 60 years, nursing expenses INR 500/day, max 10 days per hospitalization, up to 15 days per policy.

15. **Accidental Hospitalisation**
    - Sum Insured increased to 125% of the amount after claims, restricted to INR 1,00,000, payable once per insured.

16. **Child Care**
    - Attendant expenses INR 500/day, max 30 days per policy, for children below 10 years.

17. **Co-pay in Non-Network Hospitals**
    - 10% of admissible claim, in addition to excess.

18. **Convalescence Benefit**
    - INR 5,000 for hospital stay of 10+ days, once per insured, for ages 10-60.

# EXCLUSIONS

We will not pay expenses arising from or related to:

1. **Pre-existing Diseases**
   - No benefits for conditions diagnosed, treated, or symptomatic within 48 months prior to policy start, unless continuous cover for 4 years.
   - Exclusion ceases if continuous cover for specified durations (e.g., 4 years for waiver).

2. **First 30 Days of Cover**
   - Claims for diseases/illness diagnosed within 30 days of policy start, except accidental injuries.

3. **First Year of Cover (Specific Diseases)**
   - Gastric/duodenal ulcers, tonsillectomy, hernia, piles, etc., unless policy is renewed and the insured was unaware or untreated.

4. **First Two Years of Cover (Specific Diseases)**
   - Cataract, benign prostatic hypertrophy, hypertension, heart disease, diabetes, arthritis, kidney failure, etc.

5. **First Three Years of Cover (Specific Conditions)**
   - Joint replacements due to degenerative conditions, osteoarthritis, osteoporosis, etc.

6. **Treatment outside India**
7. **Epidemics recognized by WHO or Indian government**
8. **War, civil unrest, terrorism, or acts of foreign enemies**
9. **Nuclear weapons/materials**
10. **Circumcision unless for treatment or accident**
11. **Cosmetic or aesthetic treatments, plastic surgery (unless necessary for injury or deformity)**
12. **External appliances, spectacles, contact lenses, hearing aids, dentures, prostheses**
13. **Personal comfort items (TV, telephone, etc.) charged in bills**
14. **Home medical equipment (oxygen concentrators, sleep apnea devices, etc.)**
15. **Dental treatments unless due to accident**
16. **Convalescence, general debility, “run-down” condition, congenital internal/external defects**
17. **Self-injury, illegal acts, participation in unlawful activities**
18. **Substance abuse, alcohol, drug-related conditions**
19. **HIV/AIDS or related syndromes**
20. **Venereal or sexually transmitted diseases**
21. **Pregnancy, childbirth, miscarriage, abortion, complications, or elective surgeries (except ectopic pregnancy)**
22. **Fertility, sub-fertility, assisted conception, sterilization**
23. **Vaccinations, inoculations (except post-bite rabies)**
24. **Vitamins, nutritional supplements (unless prescribed for injury/disease)**
25. **Surgery for deviated septum, hypertrophied turbinates (unless due to injury)**
26. **Mental health or psychiatric conditions**
27. **Home visits by Medical Practitioners, attendant nursing unless over 60 years**
28. **Outpatient diagnostics, non-prescribed drugs, hormone therapy, sex change treatments**
29. **Hazardous activities (e.g., skiing, scuba, racing, mountaineering) unless specifically covered**
30. **Genetic disorders, stem cell procedures**
31. **Diagnosis without hospitalization or treatment not requiring hospitalization**
32. **Spa, wellness, alternative therapies (acupuncture, naturopathy, etc.)**
33. **Elective or cosmetic treatments, including plastic surgery (unless for injury/deformity)**
34. **Organ donation procedures and complications**
35. **Obesity, weight management**
36. **Experimental treatments**
37. **Donor screening or treatment costs**
38. **Treatment related to military or police duties**
39. **Service charges, surcharges, registration fees**

# GENERAL CONDITIONS

- **Due Care:** Insured must cooperate and fulfill all policy requirements; failure may lead to claim rejection.
- **Mis-description:** Misrepresentation or non-disclosure voids the policy and refunds premiums without interest.
- **Insured Person:** Only those named in the Schedule are covered; additions require endorsement and premium.
- **Package Service Expenses:** Require prior approval.
- **Communications:** Must be in writing, addressed to the insurer or insured as per schedule.
- **Unhindered Access:** Insured shall support access to medical records and attend examinations.
- **Claims Procedures:**
  - For reimbursement: Notify within 48 hours, file claim within 15 days of discharge, submit original bills, and cooperate.
  - For cashless: Follow administrator’s guidelines, submit documents as specified.
- **Cumulative Bonus:** 5% increase per claim-free year, max 25%. Claims reset bonus to nil.
- **Basis of Claims Payment:**
  - Relapse within 45 days considered same claim.
  - Claims in different locations are subject to percentage limits.
- **Multiple Policies:** Non-disclosure of other policies may lead to cancellation.
- **Fraud:** Fraudulent claims lead to forfeiture and recovery.
- **Subrogation:** Insurer may recover amounts from third parties.
- **Renewal & Cancellation:** Automatic renewal unless canceled; premiums paid on renewal are based on no change in risk.
- **Dispute Resolution:** Claims disputes are arbitrated under Indian law.
- **Medical Records:** Insurer may examine records during and after policy.
- **Geographical Limits:** Coverages are within India; claims payable in INR.
- **Observance of Terms:** Conditions must be followed; non-compliance may void claims.
- **Forfeiture:** Claims rejected and no legal action within 12 months are forfeited.
- **Post-Claim:** Sum insured reduces after claim payment.
- **Death Claims:** Paid to legal heirs or primary insured.
- **Tax Benefits:** Premiums may be tax-exempt under Section 80D.
