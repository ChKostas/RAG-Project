# A Guide to Insurance Claims

## What is an Insurance Claim?

An insurance claim is a formal request by a policyholder to an insurance company (insurer) for settlement or compensation following the occurrence of a loss event that was insured against. Making an insurance claim should be simple and stress-free, especially if you get the details right.

## What to Consider Before Making a Claim

- You should have a valid insurance policy in place.
- You must have suffered a financial loss caused by an event covered in the policy.

## Steps to Follow When Filing a Claim

### Step 1: Notify Your Insurance Provider as Soon as Practical

Inform the insurer of the details of the claim per the policy conditions and ask for what is required from you (as the claimant). The insurance provider may need documentation including, but not limited to, police records/report, witness statements, among others, to confirm that the claim is genuine.

If you purchased the insurance policy through an insurance broker, the broker will assist with the claim process. While notifying the insurer, seek clarification on:

- What is needed to have the claim paid
- How much time you have to file a claim
- How long it will take for the claim to be processed
- Whether the claim exceeds the deductible (the amount of loss you agreed to pay before insurance kicks in)

### Step 2: Prepare All Necessary Documentation

Having all the documents in place ensures quick and efficient processing. Keep copies of all relevant documents and bills related to the incident. Write detailed notes about the incident, including time, date, and the people you spoke to.

**NB:** Sometimes the insurer may require additional documents to process the claim. Make sure to provide all necessary documentation.

### Step 3: Obtain a Reference or Claim Number

After your claim has been acknowledged and registered by your insurer, get a reference or claim number to track the claim.

### Step 4: Involving a Loss Assessor

For some claims, a loss assessor may be appointed to assess the loss or damage and confirm the validity of the claim. The assessor considers the total sum insured to determine the amount payable for the specific damage or loss. Cooperate with the assessor honestly.

### Step 5: Receive a Discharge Voucher

The insurer will present a discharge voucher with the assessed amount of compensation due to you. Signing the discharge voucher, often called a settlement intimation voucher, indicates that the claim has been settled amicably.

- The discharge voucher serves as proof that the insurer’s liability is over and that the insured is satisfied with the claim amount offered for the incident.
- Upon signing, the claim is paid. The Insurance Regulatory Authority (IRA) provides guidance on timelines for payments.

### Step 6: Lodge a Complaint (If Unsatisfied)

If you receive a discharge voucher and are not satisfied, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the IRA’s Complaints Bureau and seek redress.

Common reasons for complaints include:

- Dispute over liability under policies issued
- Amount offered for settlement
- Breach of policy terms
- Undue delay in claim payment after signing the discharge voucher
- Any other matter related to the insurer’s actions

Refer to the IRA’s Complaints Bureau process for more details: [IRA Complaints Guidelines](http://www.ira.go.ug/cp/uploads/Complaints%25Guidelines.pdf)

The IRA has established claims guidelines and timelines, including:

### Claims Process and Service Standards for Insurers

| Step No. | Activity | Indicative Timelines |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Claim Intimation | As per policy conditions |
| 2 | Claim acknowledgment & document collection | Within 3 working days from intimation |
| 3 | Site visit or appointment of assessor/adjusters | - Within 3 days of claim<br>- 5 days for complex claims requiring IRA approval |
| 4 | Making settlement offer or communicating claim repudiation | 5 days from receipt of final adjustment/investigation report or final supporting documents |
| 5 | Settling Claim | - Up to Shs10 million: within 10 days after discharge voucher<br>- Above Shs10M to Shs50M: within 15 days<br>- Above Shs50M: within 20 days or upon receipt of cash call from reinsurers, whichever occurs first |

**Note:** Insurers should promptly do cash calls to reinsurers upon receiving the final adjuster’s report to avoid delays.

## Circumstances Where Your Claim May Not Be Paid

Not all claims that are intimated are payable. The following circumstances may render a claim not payable:

- The event causing the loss is not covered under the policy.
- Necessary supporting documents are not provided.
- The policy was not in force at the time of the loss.
- The policy is invalid due to misrepresentation or nondisclosure of material facts.
- The event is excluded under the policy.
- Premium payments are not up-to-date.
- The claim is exaggerated or inflated.
- The claimant failed to notify the insurer of changes in circumstances related to the policy.
- The claimant did not fulfill the conditions of the policy.

### Circumstances Where Your Claim May Not Be Paid

- The event is not covered.
- Missing or incomplete documentation.
- Policy not active at the time of loss.
- Policy invalid due to misrepresentation.
- Loss event excluded.
- Premium not paid.
- Claim inflated.
- Failure to notify insurer of relevant changes.
- Conditions of the policy not fulfilled.
